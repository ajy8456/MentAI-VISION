Usage Scenario

Service Demo Video

The My Mental Wellness Check-in service is designed for easy use in daily life through mobile devices or PCs, eliminating the need to visit hospitals or counseling centers. Unlike lengthy surveys that take over 10 minutes, users can assess their mental state by recording their voice and facial video in just 2 minutes by recording their voice and facial video.

  • Depression and Anxiety Analysis: Users record four sentences by following on-screen guides to assess depression and anxiety. To enhance the user experience, the recording volume is visualized on the screen during the process.
  • Stress Analysis: Users record a one-minute facial video to measure heart rate variability (HRV), which is used to evaluate stress levels. To ensure accuracy, the system tracks facial landmarks, such as the eyes and nose and automatically pauses recording if the user moves out of the frame.
  • Results and Trends: After the analysis, the service provides a comprehensive mental health score, along with numerical indexes for depression, anxiety and stress. These results are presented alongside group averages for reference, and users can track their mental health trends over time.
  • Personalized Recommendations: Based on the results, the service recommends tailored care programs from Happy Wings.
  • Optimal Use: For the most accurate results, the service should be used in a quiet office or home environment.
  • Data security and privacy: Our service processes sensitive user data, including voice and video inputs. To protect user privacy, all data is fully anonymized and managed under strict â€œnon-identifiableâ€ protocols. In addition, the user's input data is deleted from the server immediately after AI analysis.

Technical Details

The MentAI VISION technology used in the My Mental Wellness Check-in service analyzes depression and anxiety through an advanced AI model developed by SK Telecom (Spectral Image-Based Diagnosis of Voice Disorders: Leveraging Spectrograms for Non-Invasive Assessment, NeurIPS 2023 Workshop). It processes a userâ€™s voice by analyzing the waveform and transforming it into frequency images (spectrograms). These spectrograms are divided into image patches from multiple frequency bands, which are analyzed using computer vision techniques to classify target voice disorders.

A modified version of this model, trained on mental health data, powers the depression and anxiety analysis in the service, achieving a recall rate of approximately 89% in clinical research studies. The technology also includes language semantic analysis, leveraging speech-to-text and large language models (LLMs) tailored to specific tasksâ€”such as assessing mental health through self-introduction prompts.

To date, SK Telecom has filed 11 related domestic patents and four international patents for these technologies, including PCT/KR2022/014579, PCT/KR2024/003366, PCT/KR2024/003745, and PCT/KR2025/007063.

The stress analysis technology, developed by the partner company Clify, uses AI to predict blood flow from facial video footage. By measuring heart rate variability (HRV), it provides insights into usersâ€™ stress levels with high precision.
